Early Internet Memories


Toontown Online, Club Penguin, and Beyond

Some of my earliest memories of the internet are of playing various games on the family desktop. While I liked playing short flash games on websites like CoolMathGames.com, Poptropica, and Funbrain Playground, I was always more entranced by children's MMO (massively multiplayer online) games. I spents hours everyday on games like Club Penguin, Pixie Hollow, Petpet Park, and most of all, Toontown Online. I sunk an unfathomable amount of time running around virtual worlds, doing fetch quests, grinding in-game curency, and leveling up my accounts. To say that these companies had me hooked would be an understatement.

(Fun fact: There is a fan-run revival of Toontown Online named "Toontown Rewritten" currently ongoing. It is a complete recreation of the original Towntown. View the project here.)

Various memories:

  • Joining a "clan" in Toontown.
  • Accidentally making someone quit the aforementioned clan.
    • (He hadn't been online in a few months and asked if anyone had stolen his name yet. I answered vaguely [someone had a similar name, but not the exact same, and thus not actually stolen] but because I couldn't type quickly enough, he stormed off before I could explain.)
  • Sometimes having a naked penguin on Club Penguin because they required an active (monthly) membership to wear clothes.
  • Playing mind games on other kids at the Club Penguin Dojo (it was essentially a rock, paper, scissors minigame).
  • Downloading Pirate 101 and Wizard 101 every few months and subsequently deleting it because the paywalls made them unplayable.
